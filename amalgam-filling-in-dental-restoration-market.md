<p><p>Amalgam fillings are widely used in dental restorations, categorized primarily into low-copper and high-copper variants. Low-copper amalgam contains about 5% copper, making it more susceptible to corrosion and marginal breakdown. In contrast, high-copper amalgam, containing 10-30% copper, offers enhanced resistance to corrosion and improved longevity, reducing the risk of mercury release. The choice between these types is influenced by factors like patient needs, clinical application, and cost-effectiveness, affecting the overall market dynamics in dental restoration.</p></p>
