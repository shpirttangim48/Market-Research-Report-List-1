<p><p>The GIS software market is divided into two main types: on-premise and cloud-based solutions. On-premise GIS is installed directly on a user's hardware, offering greater control over data and customization but requiring significant upfront investment and maintenance. In contrast, cloud-based GIS leverages internet-based services for accessibility and scalability, allowing users to collaborate remotely and reduce infrastructure costs. This model facilitates easier updates and integration with other tools, making it increasingly popular among organizations seeking flexibility and cost-efficiency.</p></p>
