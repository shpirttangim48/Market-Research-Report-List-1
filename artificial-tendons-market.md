<p><p>The artificial tendons market is categorized mainly into vitro tissue and internal tissue segments. Vitro tissue involves the use of laboratory-engineered materials designed to mimic natural tendon properties, often used for research and development purposes. Internal tissue, on the other hand, refers to synthetic tendons that are implanted within the body to repair or replace damaged tendons in patients. Both types cater to different applications, with vitro focusing on testing and innovation, while internal targets clinical interventions for improved patient outcomes.</p></p>
