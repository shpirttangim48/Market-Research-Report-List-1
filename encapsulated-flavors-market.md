<p><strong>The Encapsulated Flavors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Fluid Bed Coating</li><li>Spray Drying</li><li>Glass Encapsulation</li><li>Spray Chilling/Congealing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Encapsulated flavors refer to methods used to protect and enhance flavor compounds in various food applications. The fluid bed coating technique involves coating particles with a polymer or starch to create a protective layer. Spray drying transforms liquid flavors into powdered form by evaporating moisture. Glass encapsulation uses a glass matrix to safeguard volatile flavors, while spray chilling/congealing rapidly cools liquid flavors to form solid particles. Each method ensures the stability, release, and longevity of flavors in food products.</p></p>
